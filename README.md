# Household Vehicle Ownership and Use in Sheffield MSOAs

## Project Overview

This project examines the spatial distribution of household vehicle ownership and use in Sheffield using Middle Super Output Area (MSOA) level census data. The study investigates whether residential density and household characteristics influence the number of vehicles owned by households across Sheffield.

Vehicle ownership plays a major role in shaping urban mobility patterns, traffic congestion, air quality, and city planning decisions. Understanding how vehicle ownership varies across space can help policymakers and planners design more sustainable transport systems.

Using GIS-based spatial analysis, this project visualizes patterns of:

* Household vehicle ownership
* Household composition
* Population access to vehicles
* Differences in car ownership by employment status

The project uses data from the **2011 UK Census** and spatial mapping techniques to analyse patterns across Sheffield.

# Methodology

The methodology for this project involved several key stages.

### Data Collection

Data was obtained from the **UK Census 2011** and the **Office for National Statistics (ONS)** datasets.

The datasets included:

* Number of cars or vans in households
* Household composition
* Population access to vehicles
* MSOA spatial boundary data

### Spatial Data Processing

The census datasets were joined with Sheffield MSOA spatial boundary files using GIS software. This allowed demographic and transport data to be analysed geographically.

### Mapping and Visualization

GIS mapping techniques were used to produce **choropleth maps** showing spatial patterns across the city. Data was categorized into value ranges to highlight areas with higher or lower vehicle ownership.

### Comparative Analysis

Spatial comparison was conducted between:

* household distribution
* vehicle ownership
* population access to vehicles

This helped identify potential relationships between residential density and vehicle ownership patterns.

# Key Findings

The analysis revealed several important findings:

* Vehicle ownership varies considerably across Sheffield MSOAs.
* Suburban areas generally have higher vehicle ownership.
* Higher population density areas tend to show lower vehicle ownership.
* Employment status strongly influences access to vehicles.
* Household composition also plays an important role in determining vehicle ownership patterns.

---

# Policy Implications

Understanding vehicle ownership patterns can help support:

* sustainable transport planning
* reduction of traffic congestion
* improved public transport systems
* reduction of carbon emissions in urban areas

Encouraging alternatives to private vehicle use, such as public transport, cycling, and walking, can help mitigate the negative effects of high vehicle ownership.
